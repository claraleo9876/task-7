# task-7
Learn to spot and remove potentially harmful browser extensions.
Here's a clear step-by-step guide to help you identify and remove potentially harmful browser extensions:



Step 1: **Spot Suspicious Extensions**
Look out for these red flags:
- **Unexpected behavior**: Pop-ups, redirects, or ads appearing more frequently.
- **Performance issues**: Browser slowing down or crashing.
- **Privacy concerns**: Extensions asking for excessive permissions (e.g., access to all websites, browsing history).
- **Unknown origin**: Extensions you don’t remember installing or that have vague names.

---

Step 2: **Review Installed Extensions**
#### For Chrome:
1. Click the **three dots** in the top-right corner.
2. Go to **Extensions** via `More tools > Extensions`.
3. Review the list and look for anything unfamiliar or suspicious.

#### For Firefox:
1. Click the **menu button** (three lines).
2. Select **Add-ons and themes**.
3. Go to **Extensions** and inspect each one.

#### For Edge:
1. Click the **three dots** in the top-right corner.
2. Select **Extensions**.
3. Review installed extensions.

---

Step 3: **Remove or Disable Harmful Extensions**
- Click **Remove** or **Disable** next to any extension you don’t trust or recognize.
- If unsure, **disable** first and observe if the issue resolves.

---

Step 4: **Scan for Malware**
- Use a reputable antivirus or anti-malware tool to scan your system.
- Some harmful extensions may install additional software without your knowledge.

---

Step 5: **Practice Safe Extension Habits**
- Only install extensions from official browser stores.
- Read reviews and check developer details.
- Avoid extensions with few downloads or vague descriptions.
